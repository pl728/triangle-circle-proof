A Proof that All Triangles are Circles

Theorem: All Triangles are Circles

Let \(T\) be an arbitrary triangle in the Euclidean plane \(\mathbb{R}^2\), and let \(C\) be the set of all continuous functions \(f: \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2\).

Consider the Riemannian manifold \((M, g)\) where \(M\) is a 2-dimensional differentiable manifold and \(g\) is a Riemannian metric tensor. Let \(T\) be embedded in \(M\) as a geodesic triangle.

Now, let's consider the Euler-Lagrange equation of the geodesic flow on \(M\), given by

\[\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}^i}\right) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial q^i} = 0\]

where \(L\) is the Lagrangian and \(q^i\) are generalized coordinates.

Let's define a function \(h: M \to \mathbb{R}\) such that \(h(T) = C\), where \(C\) is a circle in \(\mathbb{R}^2\).

Now, let's consider the tensor product space \(V = \mathbb{R}^2 \otimes_{\mathbb{Q}} \mathbb{R}^2\), and let \(T: V \to V\) be a linear transformation defined by

\[T(x \otimes y) = (h(x) + f(y)) \otimes (h(x) - f(y))\]

Now, by the sheer audacity of mathematical chicanery, let us postulate that

\[g(T) = h(T)\]

This concludes the proof.
